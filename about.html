Rules and Regulations


Conduct standards at the School are set forth in writing in order to give students general notice of prohibited conduct. These rules should be read broadly and are not designed to define prohibited conduct in exhaustive terms.

Any student found to have committed or to have attempted to commit the following misconduct is subjected to the disciplinary sanctions outlined. It shall not be a defense that a school official, student leader or other person authorized the behavior in question:

Section I , Section II , Section III , Section IV

Section I
SCHOOL’S GENERAL PARENT-TEAHCER’S ASSOCIATON PRESCRIBED

UNIFORM

The wearing of school uniform is not mandatory but we encourage our students to wear the prescribed uniform for their safety. Also, it promotes organization, neatness and time efficiency

  • Males
    • White polo (white shoulder piping and patch) and gray pants
    • Black shoes with plain white socks
    • School ID
  • Females
    • White polo (white shoulder piping and patch) and gray pants
    • Black shoes with plain white socks
    • School ID

HAIRCUT

  • For male students, the acceptable haircut shall be at least 2 inches above the ears and 3inches above the collar line
  • Hair color is strictly discouraged

RULES ON SCHOOL UNIFORM AND ID

  • All students are encouraged to be simple and neat inn appearance
  • Every student is encouraged to wear the prescribed school uniform at all times during the regular school days
  • The blouses of the female students and the polo of the male students must be properly buttoned. Skirts must be reasonably of decent length
  • Undershirts of male students should be in a white, not printed nor colored
  • The P.E. uniform is worn only during P.E. class

Section II
ATTENDANCE

Regular and punctual attendance is absolutely necessary. Students must attend the flag ceremony at 6:30 am every Monday and flag retreat at 2:00pm every Friday.

  • Tardiness- a student is considered tardy if he comes to class 10 minutes after the bell has rung.
  • Cutting classes- a student cuts classes if he or she willfully does not attend in one or more subject/s but present in some subjects.
  • Absenteeism
    • a. A student who has been absent is required to present to the office of the Guidance Counselor a written explanation of his absence signed by his/her parents immediately after the day he/she was absent.
    • b. A prolonged absence of at least five (5) days cause by illness must be certified with a written statement from a doctor.
    • c. When a student is absent from classes, he/she is responsible for all matters that were taken up in his/her class during his./her absences and should make all necessary arrangement for missed assignments, lessons and projects with the teacher.

Section III
NORMS OF CONDUCT

  • IN THE CAMPUS
    • All student should give due respect to the school administrators, teachers, personnel, and visitors
    • Students are expected to take care of the school properties
    • No student is allowed to loiter around the school premises
    • Students are off limits in the faculty rooms and administrative offices unless for valid reasons
  • INSIDE THE CLASSROOM
    • Silence must be strictly being observed in entering and leaving the classroom
    • If the teacher is lat, the students should review the lessons while waiting. If the teacher is absent, inform immediately the subject department head to assign a substitute
    • When a teacher or visitor enters the room, all students should erect to greet him/her
  • IN THE LIBRARY
    • A student is responsible for the reading materials he/she uses in the library. In case of losses and damages, the student will be liable for replacing them with the current cost
    • A library card must be secured for students to be able to borrow books in the library
  • IN PUBLIC PLACES AND GATHERINGS.
    • Students are expected to behave with kindness and courtesy even outside the school
    • Refrain from attracting attention by way of boisterous yelling, laughter and giggling. Speak in low voice

Section IV
SCHOOL DISCIPLINE

Disciplinary measures are employed by the PNHS to correct erreing students and for them not to repeat the mistakes, to safeguard the name of the school and to help in the progressive development of the whole studentry.

  • WARNING. The attention of the student is formally called in connection to some offense or violation.
  • PROBATION. Habitual misbehavior and unsatisfactory conduct places a student under probation for a specific period of time.
  • SUSPENSION. The student is depriving from attending classes and school activities for a determined period.
  • DROPPING/DISMISSAL. A student may be dropped in a roll if after due process was found guilty of committing a serious or major offense.
